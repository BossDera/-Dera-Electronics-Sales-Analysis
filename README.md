# Dera Electronics Sales Analysis
## Description
>  This Excel project analyzes the sales performance of Dera Electronics using transactional electronics sales data. The objective of this analysis is to generate meaningful insights that support business decision-making, revenue tracking, product performance evaluation, and regional sales monitoring.

## Project Overview
>  The dashboard provides a clean interactive interface with slicers for State, Zone, and Product Category, enabling dynamic filtering of sales data. Visualizations such as line charts, donut charts, bar charts, and scatter plots help stakeholders understand sales distribution, profit relationships, and regional performance.
>  The dashboards present key insights into:

•	Sales distribution across product categories

•	Sales trends by month

•	Sales performance by state

•Sales contribution by channel

•	Relationship between total sales and profit by product

>  Interactive slicers allow users to filter sales results by state, zone, and product category, making the dashboard flexible for business exploration.

## Steps
1. Sales by Product Category
Pivot Table:
Rows → Product Category
Values → Sales → Sum
Visualized using a donut chart to show the percentage contribution of each electronics category.

2. Sales by Channel
Pivot Table:
Rows → Channel
Values → Sales → Sum
Displayed using a stacked column chart to compare different sales channels.
Channels include:

•	Berlin

•	Catalog

•	Landline

•	Online

•	Paris

•	Reseller

3. Sales Trend by Month
Pivot Table:
Rows → Month
Values → Sales → Sum
Displayed using a line chart to highlight monthly sales patterns throughout the year.

4. Total Sales and Profit by Product
Pivot Table:
Rows → Product Name
Values:

•	Total Sales → Sum

•	Profit → Sum
Displayed using a scatter chart to show the relationship between sales and profit performance across products.

5. Sales by State
Pivot Table:
Rows → State
Values → Sales → Sum
Displayed using a column chart to compare sales performance across different states.

6. Interactive Filters (Slicers)
The dashboard includes slicers that allow users to dynamically filter the analysis.
Slicers used:

•	State

•	Zone

•	Product Category

## Findings
1.	Computers generated the highest share of sales, making it the most dominant product category.
2.	TV and Video and Cameras categories also contributed significantly to total revenue.
3.	Monthly sales trends show peaks around October and November, indicating stronger demand toward the end of the year.
4.	The scatter chart shows a strong positive relationship between sales and profit, meaning higher sales generally lead to higher profit.
5.	Ebonyi State recorded the highest sales, followed by Edo and Delta states.
6.	Some states show relatively low sales performance, indicating potential opportunities for expansion or targeted marketing.
